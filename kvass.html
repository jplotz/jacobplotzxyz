Recommendations for Kvass

Fermentation Time
While the recipe recommends letting the final mixture sit out for a week, I found that my kvass tasted much better after only 1 or 2 days of fermentation. As soon as it stops bubbling it's good to go.
Adding Fruits
Add as much fruit as you wish to kvass. As stated in the recipe, avoid citrus fruits such as oranges, lemons, grapefruit, and limes.
Sugar
Standard table sugar will make decent kvass, but the more natural you can get with your sweetener, in my experience the better the results. Try raw honey and the darkest-colored sugar cane you can get.
Black Bread
You don't have to use specifically black bread for kvass, as any bread will work. However, it is strongly recommended to use the blackest bread you can find. I have always made mine with dark rye flour from Great River Organic Milling. See my recipe here.
Container
The original recipe recommends a glass mason jar. While it has not happened to me yet, It is common to hear warnings against sealed containers for fermentation, as they will explode. I switched to using plastic 2L juice bottles with the caps partially unscrewed. You could also cover the jar with a towel or use a carboy and airlock.
Preservation
Kvass will start to taste awful after a while, so stick to making small batches that you will drink within a week.
